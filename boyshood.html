URBAN STYLE

By Paul Baker | 16/08/20

Hood style in other words Urban fashion is style born of the street. The streets as it is well known are the place to be when the sun goes down, filled with buzzing music and nightlife, but no urban dream is complete without its style. A delinquent look that emerges from the hoods as opposed to trends dictated by designers. Fashion is usually conceived by designers. Styles then filter downward to the people through channels of marketing and distribution. However urban fashion includes hip hop style of traditional fashion and often becomes a source of pride to the community or segment of the population who developed the look. Urban styles originated from the street and are sometimes copied by designers and their trends sold out in form of marketing strategies.

Street or urban style develops on the streets of the world’s major cities. Whether its the favelas of Rio, the project of the Bronx, the street of Ojuelegba, slums of Maraba or the slums of Mumbai, urban styles are everywhere and are widely ascepted by all in the community. Both old and new in the society have their unique styles associated with the urban fashion.

Anybody can wear crazy clothes and anybody can wear classic clothes. Balancing the two can be an art. If you aren’t sure what street style is, some examples includes hoodies, T-shirts, caps, jewelry, bags, sneakers, cardigan, jeans, sunglasses, leggings....
